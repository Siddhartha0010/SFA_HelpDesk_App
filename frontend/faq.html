Frequently Asked Questions

Find quick answers to common questions about SFA 2.0

How do I start my day in the SFA 2.0 app?

To start your day in the SFA 2.0 app, follow these steps:

  1. Log in to the SFA 2.0 app using your credentials
  2. On the dashboard, locate and tap the "START DAY" button
  3. Confirm your location when prompted
  4. Your day will be officially started, and you can begin your tasks

Note: You must start your day before you can record any visits or place any orders.

What is IRED score and how is it calculated?

IRED stands for In-store Retail Execution Dashboard. It's a composite score that measures the quality of execution in retail outlets. The IRED score is calculated based on the following components:

  • Availability Score (45%): Measures UF products availability along with strategic pack in the outlet
  • Cooler Score (25%): Evaluates cooler accessibility,purity and planogram
  • Activation Score (20%): Assesses promotional materials and activities
  • SOVI (10%): It is the share of visible inventory including both chilled and warm stock.

A higher IRED score indicates better in-store execution and compliance with Coca-Cola standards.

How do I place a remote order for an outlet?

To place a remote order for an outlet, follow these steps:

  1. Navigate to the "Remote Order" section from the main menu
  2. Search for the outlet by name or browse through the list
  3. Select the outlet for which you want to place an order
  4. Tap on "Place Order" or a similar button
  5. Select the products and quantities required
  6. Review the order details and confirm
  7. The order will be submitted to the system

You can track the status of your remote orders in the order history section.

How do I sync my data in the SFA 2.0 app?

To sync your data in the SFA 2.0 app, follow these steps:

  1. Ensure you have an active internet connection
  2. Navigate to the main menu or dashboard
  3. Look for the "SYNC DATA" button or option
  4. Tap on it to initiate the sync process
  5. Wait for the sync to complete (this may take a few moments)
  6. You'll see a confirmation message once the sync is successful

It's recommended to sync your data regularly, especially at the start and end of your day, to ensure all information is up-to-date.

What should I do if I encounter technical issues with the app?

If you encounter technical issues with the SFA 2.0 app, follow these troubleshooting steps:

  1. Restart the app to see if the issue resolves
  2. Check your internet connection
  3. Clear the app cache (through your device settings)
  4. Ensure you have the latest version of the app installed
  5. Restart your device

If the issue persists, you can:

  • Use the in-app "Chat Support" feature to connect with technical support
  • Contact your supervisor or IT support team
  • Submit a detailed description of the issue through the help desk portal

Remember to note the specific error messages or behaviors you're experiencing to help with troubleshooting.

How do I end my day in the SFA 2.0 app?

To end your day in the SFA 2.0 app, follow these steps:

  1. Complete all your pending tasks and ensure all data is synced
  2. Navigate to the dashboard
  3. Locate and tap the "END DAY" button
  4. Confirm your action when prompted
  5. The app will finalize your day's activities and submit the data to the server

Note: It's important to properly end your day to ensure all your activities are recorded and your performance metrics are updated correctly.

How can I improve my route efficiency?

To improve your route efficiency in the SFA 2.0 app, consider these tips:

  • Plan your route in advance using the Route Planning feature
  • Group outlets by proximity to minimize travel time
  • Consider traffic patterns and peak hours when planning your visits
  • Prioritize outlets based on sales potential and visit frequency requirements
  • Use the map view to visualize your route and make adjustments as needed
  • Regularly review your route efficiency metrics to identify areas for improvement

By optimizing your route, you can visit more outlets in less time, increasing your productivity and sales opportunities.

How do I track my incentives and rewards?

To track your incentives and rewards in the SFA 2.0 app:

What is the difference between the various store categories?

The SFA 2.0 app categorizes stores into different tiers based on their sales volume, strategic importance, and other factors:

  • Platinum: Highest tier stores with the largest sales volume and strategic importance. These outlets require priority attention and frequent visits.
  • Gold: High-performing stores with significant sales potential. These outlets are important for achieving sales targets.
  • Silver: Mid-tier stores with moderate sales volume. These outlets contribute steadily to overall sales.
  • Bronze: Lower-tier stores with smaller sales volume. These outlets may require development to increase their potential.

Additionally, stores are classified by type (Traditional Kirana, Convenience, End Standing, etc.) to help tailor your sales approach based on the outlet's characteristics.
